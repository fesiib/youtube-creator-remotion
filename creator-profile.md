## 3. Voice & writing

This channel runs with **no narration and minimal subtitles** by default. Text is a supporting tool for mood, not a vehicle for information.

- **Register:** Cinematic and serious. The goal is "the flashiest, most serious action scene possible."

- **Signature phrases or verbal tics:** Onomatopoeia is allowed to emphasize impact (e.g. "퍽!", "쾅!"), but rendered with a cinematic feel rather than a comic-book one.

## 7. Visual treatment

- **Color treatment:**
  - **Base:** White background with black/gray ink — monochrome.
  - **Hero:** Near-black (`#0a0a0a`). The protagonist anchors the eye with deep black.
  - **Enemy:** Mid gray (`#9c9c9c`). Enemies are distinguished by color tone. Character identity is read by BOTH position and color tone.
  - **Accent (reserved colors):** Used only at impact moments — technique activation, explosions, K.O. hits, etc. Candidates: red (`#e23030`), orange, yellow. Never used during everyday frames.
- **Text-on-screen style:** Minimal, cinematic. Subtitles are rarely used. Onomatopoeia only at impact moments. Dark gray / black text on the white background.
- **Motion graphics:** _(unsettled — motion blur / speed lines / impact flashes are encouraged when they serve the impact)_
- **Zoom / punch-in usage:** Use aggressively. Within a single scene, change zoom and angle between cuts to keep things dynamic.
- **Composition rule (emphasized):** Vary compositions and angles. Don't sit on one static wide shot. Keep things dynamic with camera moves, cuts, and angle changes.
- **Readability rule (emphasized):** The viewer must always be able to follow what is happening. Hold dynamism and readability simultaneously. Characters must be distinguishable from each other by color and position.
- **Pose-direction rule (emphasized):** A character's pose must clearly read which direction they are facing. Matching only their on-screen left/right position is not enough — torso lean, foot placement, and arm direction must all line up as body language that points "toward the opponent." Ambiguous poses make the viewer wonder "is this character facing away from me?"
- **Ground contact rule (emphasized):** Characters' feet should touch the ground by default. Even with bent legs, the visual sole must meet the ground line. Intentionally airborne motion (jumps, aerial combos, blown-back K.O.s) is fine — when it happens, "off the ground" must be a deliberate design choice.
